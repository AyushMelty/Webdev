About DREAMRS Lab

The Design Research and Human Factors Lab (DREAMRS Lab) is a research hub dedicated to exploring how designers approach challenges, process information, and innovate. The major focus is design thinking, an intricate process involving cognition, physiology, and neuroscience. This research framework dives into how designers think, make decisions, solve problems and generate innovative ideas offering actionable insights to improve design practices which further impacts human experiences, behaviors, and performance in various contexts, ranging from consumer products to complex systems.

The lab focuses on how designers create solutions that optimize usability, safety, efficiency, feasibility and overall user satisfaction. These insights fuel innovative approaches to design that cater not only to functional requirements but also to the emotional and cognitive needs of users.

The lab's research leverages both qualitative and quantitative methodologies, including usability testing, cognitive task analysis, and data-driven design optimizations. Through a multidisciplinary approach, DREAMRS explores the deep connection between human behavior and the designed world, ultimately striving to create better, more intuitive interactions.

Design Research

Our lab is dedicated to exploring innovative approaches to design. Through comprehensive studies of creative and analytical processes, we aim to improve the ways products, services, and systems are conceptualized and developed. By focusing on design creativity, effective tools, and ergonomic principles, we ensure that our research drives meaningful advancements in the field of design.

Human Factors

We focus on the study of human behavior, capabilities, and limitations to ensure that our designs align with the needs of users. By integrating physical and cognitive ergonomics, our research addresses critical aspects of safety, performance, and well-being. This ensures that the systems and products we develop are optimized for real-world applications, delivering both functionality and user satisfaction.
